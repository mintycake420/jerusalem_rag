...reduced to the petty proportions of a fiscal system. The **jurisconsults** [legal experts] of this land were thus able to remain within the realm of great speculative and historical questions, thereby ensuring for their writings a lasting success.

One perceiveth that in France several causes, independent of the merit of the historians and the jurisconsults of **feudalism** [the system of land tenure and vassalage], have altered the character of their writings; and that this matter, so worthy of study, yet calleth for the attention of grave and enlightened minds, who henceforth have naught to fear from the impulses or prejudices of public opinion. But the more the times be favorable to a calm and reflected examination of those political questions which did excite, in such diverse ways, the passions of our predecessors, the more imperious becometh the necessity to seek the solution thereof in the purest historical sources, and to refuse all credit to those systematic ideas—those so-called new and ingenious insights—which excite curiosity only by means of paradox or the oddities of their form. 

Henceforth, no historian, no **publicist** [a writer on public law or politics], shall place his ideas under the patronage of party spirit; nor can he hope to fix, even for a moment, attention upon these grave matters, if he joineth not to that erudition which gathereth testimonies with impartiality, the talent of thought and writing which bringeth them to fruition. Numerous publications of historical documents, undertaken by order of the government or by private individuals, bear witness that these truths have become well-known in France; and the **Royal Academy of Inscriptions and Belles-Lettres**, by ordaining that the most precious work of feudal jurisprudence be brought to light in its full extent and with greater care than its first editors bestowed, hath shown that she was not content merely to approve this wise direction of minds, but that she wished to give to each of the parts which compose the vast domain of our history...
